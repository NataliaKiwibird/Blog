CSS Types of position

static

Keeps an element in normal document flow of the webpage.

position: static;

Useful when your document flow is modified by other elements.

sticky

A mixture of static and fixed. Element is static at beginning and becoming fixed while scrolling.

position: sticky;
top: 0;

You need to specify at least one of top/ right/ bottom/ left.

fixed

It positions a certain element relative to its viewport. Often used for navigation bars and social media icons.

position: fixed;
top: 0;
left: 0;

element positioned in the upper left corner.

Specify top/ right/ bottom/ left for positioning element.

relative

It shifts an element around relative its original position.

position: relative;
left: 20px;
bottom: 20px;

moves an element away by 20px left and down to its original position

You need to specify top/ right/ bottom/ left for positioning the element.

absolute

It positions an element absolutely relative to its closest parent (usuallu it's a <div= containing the element).

position: absolute;
left: 20px;
bottom: 20px;

moves away by 20px from left and bottom of a parent element

Specify top/ right/ bottom/ left. The parent needs to get position: relative!
