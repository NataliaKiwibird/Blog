Inline and block elements

A differnece between them is how they are displayed in a browser and how much room they take upon a page.

Inline elements

They line up next to each other and they don't take more room than their content needs.

Examples:

a, abbr, acronym, b, bdo, big, br, button, cite, code, dfn, em, i, img, input, kbd, label, map, object, output, q, samp, script, select, small, span, strong, sub, sup, textarea, time, tt, var.

View:

<span> <img> <a href> <input> <button> <label> <textarea>

Block elements

They take up the whole width of a page regardless of content. They always style in a new line.

Examples:

address, article, aside, blockquote, canvas, dd, div, dl, dt, fieldset, figcaption, figure, footer, form, h1-h6, header, hr, li, main, nav, noscript, ol, p, pre, section, table, tfoot ul, video.

View:

<div>
<header>
<p>
<ul>
<li>
